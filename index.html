Who we are

METARA exists to help discover, design and implement the patterns that already sustain life — and apply them to how we build economies, communities and ecosystems.

Life has demonstrated through billions of years of evolution that resilient systems emerge through cooperation, diversity, specialization, and interconnectedness. A healthy forest thrives not because every tree competes for the most resources — but because countless relationships continuously create more life than they consume. METARA is built on that same principle.

"To cultivate a global network of thriving regenerative communities that demonstrate how humanity can flourish in partnership with nature."

The ecosystem

A living system of enterprises

  • Growing

    METARA Living Solutions

    The commercial engine — financially sustainable businesses that solve real-world problems while generating the resources that power the wider ecosystem.

  • Growing — Pilot Phase

    METARA Collaboration & Eco Solutions

    A local circular economy platform connecting independent businesses in Plettenberg Bay into a mutually beneficial network — turning waste into resource, reducing costs, and building community.

  • Seed · Awaiting Fertile Ground

Circular economy

Waste is not worthless

Waste is accumulated embodied energy, labour, materials, knowledge and ecological extraction that has become disconnected from a useful cycle. METARA exists to close those cycles — and reward the people who do it.

↻

Collect

Resources are recovered from waste streams across the local economy

◈

Credit

Members earn Resource Credits for every regenerative contribution

⬡

Produce

The cooperative transforms materials into products, food, energy and services

◎

Circulate

Credits flow locally, strengthening the bioregional economy with every cycle

"A living economy where contribution to the commons becomes the foundation of prosperity."

METARA Circular Economy

The first pilot is launching now in Plettenberg Bay — connecting a small cluster of local businesses into a food and soil loop: coffee waste to mushroom substrate, spent substrate to compost, compost to vegetables, vegetables back to the café. Simple. Proven. Ready to expand.
